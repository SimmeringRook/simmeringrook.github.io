## Question 20

Convert each temperature.

### Part A

?> $212 \,^{\circ}F$ to $\,^{\circ}C$

$$\begin{aligned}
\,^{\circ}C &= \frac{\,^{\circ}F - 32}{1.8}\\
&= \frac{212\,^{\circ}F - 32}{1.8} \\
&= 100 \,^{\circ}C
\end{aligned}$$

### Part B

?> $22 \,^{\circ}C$ to $K$

$$\begin{aligned}
K &= \,^{\circ}C + 273.15\\
&= 295.15 K \\
&= 300 K = 3.0 \times 10^2 K
\end{aligned}$$

### Part C

?> $0.00 K$ to $\,^{\circ}F$

$$\begin{aligned}
K &= \,^{\circ}C + 273.15 \\
&= \frac{\,^{\circ}F - 32}{1.8} + 273.15\\
\,^{\circ}F &= \frac{5}{9}(K - 273.15)+32\\
\\
&= 1.8(0.00K - 273.15)+32\\
&= -459.67 \,^{\circ}F = -460 \,^{\circ}F = -4.60\times 10^2\,^{\circ}F
\end{aligned}$$

### Part D

?> $2.735 K$ to $\,^{\circ}C$

$$\begin{aligned}
\,^{\circ}C &= K - 273.15\\
\\
&= 2.735 K - 273.15 \\
&= -270.415 \,^{\circ}C = -2.704 \times 10^2 \,^{\circ}C
\end{aligned}$$

## Question 39

For each number, underline the zeroes that are significant and draw and $x$ through the zeroes that are not.

### Part A

?> $1,050,501\ km$

All zeroes that are to the left of the decimal point are bounded by non-zero digits, therefore all $0$ are significant. In Scientific Notation, this length is written as: $1.050501\times 10^{6}\ km$.

### Part B

?> $0.0020\ m$

Rewriting the given number in Scientific Notation, we obtain: $2.0\times 10^{-3}\ m$, and so we see that the zeroes preceding the $2$ are insignificant, while the zero immediately following the $2$ is significant.

### Part C

?> $0.000000000000002\ s$

Again, rewriting the given number in Scientific Notation, we obtain: $2\times 10^{-15}\ s$, and so we see that all of the zeroes present in the original time measurement are insignificant.

### Part D

?> $0.001090\ cm$

In Scientific Notation: $1.090\times 10^{-3}\ cm$; therefore, the first 3 zeros "$0.00$" are insignificant while the zeroes contained in $1090$ are significant.

## Question 57

Ethylene glycol (antifreeze) has a density of $1.11\ g/cm^3$.

>Recall: $$\rho = \frac{m}{V}$$

### Part A

?> What is the mass, in $g$, of $417\ mL$ of this liquid?

Therefore, the mass of $417\ mL$ is:

$$\begin{aligned}
m &= \rho V\\
&= \left(\frac{1.11\ g}{cm^3}\right) \left(\frac{417\ mL}{1}\cdot\frac{1\ cm^3}{1\ mL}\right)\\
&= 462.87\ g\\
&= 4.63\times 10^2\ g
\end{aligned}$$

### Part B

?> What is the volume, in $L$, of $4.1\ kg$ of this liquid?

Rearranging our expression:

$$\begin{aligned}
\rho &= \frac{m}{V}\\
V &= \frac{m}{\rho}
\end{aligned}$$

Therefore, the volume of $4.1\ kg$ is:

$$\begin{aligned}
V &= \frac{m}{\rho}\\
&= \left(\frac{4.1\ kg}{1}\cdot\frac{10^3\ g}{1\ kg}\right) \left(\frac{1\ cm^3}{1.11\ g}\frac{1\ L}{10^3\ cm^3}\right)\\
&= \frac{4.1}{1.11}\times \frac{10^3}{10^3}\ L\\
&= 3.\overline{693}\ L\\
&= 3.7\ L
\end{aligned}$$

# E.7: Unit Conversions

## Question 61

Perform each unit conversion.

### Part A

?> $27.8\ L$ to $cm^3$

Significant Figures: 3

$$\begin{aligned}
1\ L &= 10^{3}\ cm^3\\
\\
\frac{27.8\ L}{1} \frac{10^{3}\ cm^3}{L} &= 27.8 \times 10^{3}\ cm^3 \\
&= 2.78\times 10^4\ cm^3
\end{aligned}$$

### Part B

?> $1898\ mg$ to $kg$

Significant Figures: 4

$$\begin{aligned}
1\ g = 10^{3}\ mg, &\quad 1\ kg = 10^3\ g\\
\\
\frac{1898\ mg}{1}\cdot\frac{1\ g}{10^3\ mg}\cdot\frac{1\ kg}{10^3\ g} &= 1898 \times 10^{-6}\ kg \\
&= 1.898\times 10^{-3}\ kg
\end{aligned}$$

### Part C

?> $198\ km$ to $cm$

Significant Figures: 3

$$\begin{aligned}
1\ km = 10^{3}\ m, &\quad 1\ m = 10^2\ cm\\
\\
\frac{198\ km}{1}\cdot\frac{10^3\ m}{1\ km}\cdot\frac{10^2\ cm}{1\ m} &= 198 \times 10^{5}\ cm\\
&= 1.98\times 10^{7}\ cm
\end{aligned}$$

## Question 69

A house has an area of $195\ m^2$. What is its area in:

### Part A

?> $km^2$

Significant Figures: 3

$$\begin{aligned}
1\ m = 10^{-3} km, &\quad 1\ m^2 = 10^{-6}\ km^2 \\
\\
\frac{195\ m^2}{1} \frac{10^{-6}\ km^2}{m^2} &= 195 \times 10^{-6}\ km^2 \\
&= 1.95\times 10^{-4}\ km^2
\end{aligned}$$

### Part B

?> $dm^2$

Significant Figures: 3

$$\begin{aligned}
1\ m = 10^{1} dm, &\quad 1\ m^2 = 10^{2}\ dm^2 \\
\\
\frac{195\ m^2}{1} \frac{10^{2}\ dm^2}{m^2} &= 195 \times 10^{2}\ dm^2 \\
&= 1.95\times 10^{4}\ dm^2
\end{aligned}$$

### Part C

?> $cm^2$

Significant Figures: 3

$$\begin{aligned}
1\ m = 10^{2} cm, &\quad 1\ m^2 = 10^{4}\ cm^2 \\
\\
\frac{195\ m^2}{1} \frac{10^{4}\ cm^2}{m^2} &= 195 \times 10^{4}\ cm^2 \\
&= 1.95\times 10^{6}\ cm^2
\end{aligned}$$
